G. Dramborleg pn. “Thudder-sharp”

@@@ The axe of Tuor, translated “Thudder-sharp”, appearing in @@@ (UT/172). This name appears to be a combination of drambor “blow (with a fist)” and laeg² “sharp”.

Conceptual Development: The only other appearance of this name was G. Dramborleg is earliest Lost Tales, where it was also translated “Thudder-sharp” (LT2/203). This could be an example of Tolkien restoring an old name without updating its etymology. However, the element N. drambor “clenched fist; blow with a fist” appears in the Etymologies from the 1930s (Ety/DARÁM), so perhaps this name is an example of a word that retained its form and meaning from Tolkien’s earliest to latest writings.
