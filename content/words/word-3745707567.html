      Q. ahto n. “wright, maker” (Category: Artisan, Craftsman)

      A word for “maker, wright” used as a suffix in the first version of Tengwesta Qenderinwa (TQ1) from the 1930s, derived from ᴹ✶(a)k’tō as an abnormal vocalization of the root ᴹ√KAT “make” (PE18/62). The word ahto reappeared in the second version of Tengwesta Qenderinwa (TQ2) from around 1950 as an independent word with the same meaning and derivation (PE18/85). The section in TQ2 containing this word was marked through, but the logic for its derivation remains valid, so I would retain ᴺQ. ahto “wright, maker” for purposes of Neo-Quenya.
