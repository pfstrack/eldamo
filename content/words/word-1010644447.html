Q. Ilúvatar m. “Father of All”

A Quenya name for God (S/15, Let/155), translated “Father of All” or “All-father” (Let/204, MR/39). It is a compound of the noun ilúvë “all” and atar¹ “father” (SA/atar, ilúvë).

Conceptual Development: This name dates back to the earliest Lost Tales (LT1/49), though ᴱQ. Ilúvatar originally meant “Sky Father” or “Heavenly Father” (LTA1/Ilúvatar, QL/42). The translation “All-father” first emerged in Silmarillion drafts from the 1930s (SM/78, LR/110), and ᴹQ. Ilúvatar appeared in the Etymologies as the combination of ᴹQ. ilúve and ᴹQ. atar (Ety/ATA, IL).
