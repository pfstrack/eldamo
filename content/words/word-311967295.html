√THĀ/ATHA root. “ease, comfort, heal; be helpful; be willing to assist (in any work, etc.), agree, consent”

References ✧ PE17/49, 148, 172; PE22/165-166

Glosses

  • “be helpful; be willing to assist (in any work, etc.), agree, consent” ✧ PE22/165 (ÞĀ̆/AÞA)
  • “be willing, agree, consent, grant; assist, join with others[?]” ✧ PE22/166 (ATHA)
  • “ease, comfort, heal” ✧ PE17/49 (ATH); PE17/148 (ATH)

Variations

  • ATH ✧ PE17/49; PE17/148
  • ATHA ✧ PE17/148; PE22/166
  • AÞA ✧ PE17/172
  • ÞĀ̆/AÞA ✧ PE22/165

Related

  • ÞĀ̆/AÞA “be helpful; be willing to assist (in any work, etc.), agree, consent” though this sense is obscured in the uses of aþa- with other verbs, it [“be helpful”] may be supposed to be the earliest sense, because of derivatives made from it, such as Q. aþea ✧ PE22/165
  • ÞĀ̆/AÞA “be helpful; be willing to assist (in any work, etc.), agree, consent” positive of ABA “refuse” ✧ PE22/165
  • THĀ/ATHA “ease, comfort, heal; be helpful; be willing to assist (in any work, etc.), agree, consent” vs. HATH “treat kindly/make easy, (help to) cure”
  • HATHA “treat kindly/make easy, (help to) cure” [as opposed to] ATHA “be willing, agree, consent, grant; assist, join with others[?]” ✧ PE22/166
