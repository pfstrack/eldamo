Ilk. voiceless stops voiced after vowels; [V{ptk}] > [V{bdg}]

As described in the Comparative Tables, voiceless stop were voiced medially (PE19/23), which was also the rule in Noldorin. From the examples in the Etymologies, it is clear this change occurred consistently after vowels. Some clear examples are: ᴹ✶mapā > Ilk. mâb (Ety/MAP), ᴹ✶atar > Ilk. adar (Ety/ATA) and ᴹ✶tiukā > Ilk. tiog (Ety/TIW). This development must have occurred after voiced stops spirantalized after vowels, since the voiced stops resulting from this rule did not further develop into spirants.

Order (04400)

After 04300 voiced stops spirantalized after vowels ᴹ✶atar > Ilk. adar Ety/ATA
Before 04600 [w] vanished after medial velars ᴹ✶-kw- > Ilk. -g(w)- PE19/23
