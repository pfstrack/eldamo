S. Imrath Gondraich loc. “Stonewain Valley”

A Sindarin name for the Stonewain Valley, written as Imrath Gondraich by Christoper Tolkien in the index of the Unfinished Tales (UTI/Stonewain Valley), and as Imrath Gondraith by Hammond and Scull based on Tolkien’s “Unfinished Index” of The Lord of the Rings (RC/558). The first word is imrath “valley with a road” (RC/558), and the initial element of the second word is clearly gond “stone”, so the final element raich/raith must be “wain”, perhaps a variant of the attested Sindarin word for “wagon” rasg¹, appearing in the other name for the Stonewain Valley: Nan Gondresgion (PE17/28).
