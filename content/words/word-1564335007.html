S. sant, n. “garden, field, yard” (Category: Garden, Orchard)

A word appearing in The Rivers and Beacon-hills of Gondor from the end of the 1960s, which meant “a garden, field, yard, or other place in private ownership, whether enclosed or not” (VT42/20). It was derived from √SAT “place, space” (VT42/19).

Conceptual Development: The Gnomish Lexicon of the 1910s had G. tand {“fence, border, bound” >>} “enclosure, garden” based on the early root ᴱ√tadh- (GL/68).
