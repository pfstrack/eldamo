S. Tharbad loc. “Crossway, Crossing”

A ford over the river Gwathló (LotR/274) translated as “Crossway” (SA/thar) or “(Road) Crossing” (RC/15, PE17/34). This name is a combination of thar “across” and pâd “way” (PE17/34).

Conceptual Development: The name N. Tharbad appeared in Lord of the Rings drafts from the 1940s (TI/164). It also appeared in Etymologies from the 1930s as a derivative of the root ᴹ√THAR “across, beyond”, where it had a difficult-to-read gloss, most likely “*Crossway” according to Christopher Tolkien (Ety/THAR).
