S. Ulfast m.

Son of the traitorous Easterling chief Ulfang (S/157). This name seems to be Sindarin, and therefore was probably not his true name. It seems to be a combination of ul- “*ugly” and fast “shaggy hair”.

Conceptual Development: This character was named N. Ulfast in Silmarillion drafts from the 1930s (SM/300, LR/287) and appeared in the Etymologies as a combination of ᴹ√ÚLUG and N. fast, the source of the derivation given above. (Ety/ÚLUG, PHAS).
