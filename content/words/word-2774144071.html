Q. [ᴱQ.] Markirya “The Last Ark”

A Quenya poem that appears in the Monsters and the Critics (MC/221-2). Tolkien wrote the first version of this poem, Oilima Markirya “The Last Ark”, prior to 1931. Roughly four decades later, he wrote another version, towards the end of his life, after the linguistic concepts of his languages had matured.

This version of the poem had no title, but in the literature it is usually referred to at the Markirya poem, since the word ᴱQ. oilima is unlikely to be valid in later Quenya, but Markirya could be (“home-ship?”). The Quenya words in the text presented here are from the 2nd version of the poem on MC/221-2, but without the revisions noted by Christopher Tolkien at the end of the poem, which present some problems of interpretation.

As noted by Christopher Tolkien (MC/223), while the Elvish text was almost completely revised, the meaning of the poem was nearly identical to the version from four decades earlier. The English glosses here are from the translation of the earlier version of the poem on MC/214, with the modifications in lines 33-34 as indicated by Christopher Tolkien in note 8, MC/220 (“green rocks” >> “dark rocks”, “red skies” >> “ruined skies”). There is also an editorial addition of a gloss for the word ve in line 20: “like”, as it is in line 5.

The text is divided into phrases for each line of the poem, except for lines 29-30 (elenillor pella talta-taltala) which are combined to make a more complete phrase. Other modifications and textual history are discuss in the entries for individual phrase.
