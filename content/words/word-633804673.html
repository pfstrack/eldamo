Q. word-order grammar.

Ordinary word order in Quenya is quite similar to English:

  • The ordinary sentence order is subject-verb-object (PE17/72).
  • Adjectives precede the nouns they modify (PE17/93, PM/346).
  • Prepositions and relative pronouns appear at the beginning of subordinate clauses.

Some of these rules were described by Tolkien. He stated that “the classical and normal order was expressed subject, verb, object” (PE17/72) and “Quenya preferred the (older) order in which adjectival stems preceded” (PM/346). Most of these discussions are part of descriptions to exceptions of these rules, however. @@@
